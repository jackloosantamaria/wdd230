Visual Hierarchy

Amazon

Amazon.com

Visual Hierarchy is the principle of arranging elements to show their order of importance. The visual characteristics help the users understand information easily. It influences users' perceptions and guide them to desired actions. Amazon is a good example of Visual Hierarchy as its homepage uses a clear Visual Hierarchy to guide users through varios sections.

Hick's Law

Dell

Dell.com

Hick's Law is a principle that says that the more choices you present your user with, the longer it will take them to reach a decision. This principle make our products more intuitive to navigate and reduce the time to value. By prioritzing the most important features we avoid overwhelming the user and reduce their reaction time. Dell is a good example of Hick's Law as they have priotize their most important products and service to reduce their user's reaction time.

Fitt's Law

Spotify.com

Fitt's Law is a principle that suggests that the most important and frequently used elements should be large and placed in easily accessible positions. This reduces effort and interaction time, thus improving usability and efficiency. Spotify is a good example of this principle since its most important elements are large in size, which makes it easier to select music while reducing effort and discomfort.
